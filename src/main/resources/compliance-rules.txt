POLICY-KYC-001: Government ID Requirements
Acceptable government IDs for KYC verification include Passport, Driver License, and State ID. All IDs must be valid and not expired. Documents expired more than 30 days are rejected. Both front and back of ID must be submitted.

POLICY-KYC-002: Address Proof Requirements
Valid address proof documents include Utility bills, Bank statements, Lease agreements, and Government mail. Address proof must be less than 3 months old. PO Box addresses are not acceptable for primary residence verification.

POLICY-KYC-003: Income Verification Requirements
Acceptable income documents include Pay stubs from last 30 days, W2 forms from last tax year, Employment letter on company letterhead, and Bank statements showing regular deposits. Self employed customers must provide 2 years tax returns.

POLICY-KYC-004: Joint Account Requirements
Joint account opening requires government ID from all applicants. Each applicant SSN must be verified separately. Combined income proof required. All applicants must sign account agreement. Minor joint accounts require guardian documentation.

POLICY-KYC-005: SSN Handling Policy
Customer SSN must never be stored in plain text. SSN must be encrypted using AES-256 encryption before database storage. SSN must be masked in all UI displays showing only last 4 digits. SSN transmission must use TLS 1.3 or higher.

POLICY-KYC-006: NRI Account Requirements
Non Resident Indian accounts require valid Passport with visa, Overseas address proof, Indian PAN card, Foreign bank statement for last 6 months, and Source of funds declaration. NRI accounts require enhanced due diligence.

POLICY-AML-001: Cash Transaction Reporting
Cash deposits or withdrawals exceeding $10,000 must be reported to FinCEN using CTR form within 15 days. Structured transactions designed to avoid $10,000 threshold are illegal and must be reported as suspicious activity.

POLICY-AML-002: Suspicious Activity Reporting
Suspicious Activity Reports must be filed within 30 days of detecting suspicious activity. SAR must be filed for transactions with no apparent lawful purpose. Bank employees must not tip off customers about SAR filing.

POLICY-AML-003: High Risk Customer Due Diligence
Politically Exposed Persons require enhanced due diligence. High risk countries require additional verification. Customers with large unexplained wealth require source of funds documentation. High risk customers reviewed quarterly.

POLICY-PCI-001: Card Data Security
Full credit card numbers must never be stored. CVV must never be stored after transaction authorization. Card numbers must be masked showing only last 4 digits. PCI-DSS compliance required for all card data handling.

POLICY-GDPR-001: Data Privacy Requirements
Customer personal data must be collected only for specified purposes. Data retention period is 7 years after account closure. Customers have right to request data deletion subject to regulatory requirements. Data breach must be reported within 72 hours.

POLICY-FRAUD-001: Identity Fraud Prevention
Document tampering detected by inconsistent fonts, altered dates, or mismatched information results in immediate rejection. Suspected fraud cases must be escalated to fraud investigation team within 1 hour. Law enforcement notification required for confirmed fraud.
